PbNation

PbNation is a free english based forum known to have more user traffic than all other paintball media combined.

View site

ABOUT

Being user generated, the site offers field and store directory, product reviews, a market for buying, selling, and trading, a place to organize events, and a great place to share information, pictures and videos.

MY ROLE

Being a solo project, I did everything from the user interviews, user stories, creating the personaâ€™s, site mapping and flow charts, to iterating and testing of wireframes and mockups, all the way up to the prototype version. Oh, and I also did the rebranding of the logo.

OBJECTIVES

After gathering user feedback from interviews, the primary goals of the redesign and rebrand were to:

  • Focus on allowing users to find content faster; increasing user efficiency
  • Allow users a better way to save, follow and favorite threads
  • Visually and mentally, create a more comprehensive design layout
  • Update the sites appearance with a more modern and clean look
  • Give users a better option for buying, selling, and trading
  • Give users a way to create events

INITIAL RESEARCH

The first test is a survey made to know how the site is being interacted with and understand what the opiniions of the users were. Knowing what areas are being used most will allow me to test them and to focus on them specifically to render a better user experience. The second test used was to gauge how efficient the site functions overall. Using time and verbal feedback as measurement, testing navigation to different sections of the site will open up potential issues and will set a baseline when checking the disparity between the old and redesign down the road.

Survey

The survey consisted of asking questions about what part of the site they used most and if that part of the site was created well and served its purpose. The feedback gathered displayed the following:
  • 77% of respondants said they have used the buying, selling, and trading sub-forum
  • Almost 100% said they have used it to review products or have given a review
  • 43.3% Use the site to schedule events, team practices, or tryouts
  • 15.5% of people said they have posted video or pictures of events and nearly 100% have said they have used the forums to look at them
  • 22.6% consistently (weekly) use the forums for paintball news
  • 100% of people have added a friend to their buddy list
The most mentioned problems with the site were:
  • Viewing/finding a buddies profile
  • Finding self-created thread(s)
  • Navigating through a thread in BST (Buying, Selling, Trading - a user coming to potentially buy products from another user has no easy way of knowing if that product has been sold)
  • Organizing events
  • No way of creating poles for surveying
Based off the responses, it was quite simple to narrow down what areas needed improving. But to understand if my redesign would be successful, I wanted to get a baseline of the original site.

Efficiency Test

Using a scale of one through ten, I invited multiple people ranging from very experienced, a ten, to some experience, to no experience at all and used time as well as verbal feedback as a way of gauging efficiency.

Test 1 - find your account
  • 80% of people took over 2 minutes to do
  • 30% of people did not finish
Most peoples verbal feedback communicated very clearly that they were unable to find how to access their account. Most were able to eventually find it through radom clicking on the navigation bar - located under User CP (User Control Panel) and once there, under settings & options.

Test 2 - Add someone to your buddy list
  • The average time it took to complete the test was 2 minutes and 24 seconds
  • 20% of people did not finish
Most people said that they did not know how to specifically find another user at first. The 'universal' search bar did nothing for them. Most couldn't understand why the search bar for finding and adding another user is located inside their control panel.

Test 3 - Favorite/Follow a thread
  • The average time it took to complete the test was 2 minutes and 43 seconds
  • 50% took over 4 minutes
  • 20% of people did not finish
It was obvious that testers who had more experience were able to do this task with lesser difficulty, but all responded by saying that the task should not of been as difficult as it was. It was also said that the amount of hypertext made the task much more confusing. Thus the people with less forum experience struggled with this task often getting lost.

Among bad user feedback on the original site, one of the other large complications that hindered the users experience was the sites search system. Between data entries, unless you were a paying memeber, there was a 30 second lockout before searching again.

PERSONAS

PbNation is a sport tailored mainly to a male audience between ages 12 and 24 but does include females and people of all ages. For this reason, personas were made to resemble this demgographic. Making sure every page I redesigned, or a new feature that I was building into the site, was built to the users expectations. For this reason and because of the shear size of the site, having the personas to refer back to was very useful.

    PREFERENCE TESTING

    Once the sites functionality and efficiency were addressed, I focused on running preference tests for visual design. Some of the sites bigger visual issues were the abundance of hypertext, a subpar cookie-crumb navigation, and the color palette. As a result, there was no clear navigation. Nothing stood out or seemed to show any signs of importance or differentiation. It was boring.

    The goal for this was to maintain a clean and professional look but add a touch color and character. It was important to create clear navigational paths, but also represent the world of paintball.

    RESULTS

    Successfully implementing an improved, more functional, and more efficient site, I believe would make a positive change for users. It brings together some of the sites older elements with some newer ones but also allows the user to engage with the content more by reducing wait time, increasing site spatial awareness, and as an extra, improving visitor retention time.
