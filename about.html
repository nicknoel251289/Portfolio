Who am I?!

My journey is a long one, but whose isn’t. I’ll start by saying, I LOVE drawing! I’m pretty sure I popped out with a pad and pen in my hands. Growing up, I was constantly drawing. Whether it was random doodles or the trendiest Pokémon, I couldn’t put the pencil down.

Upon graduating from college with a BFA, I realized there wasn’t much I could do with just a fine arts degree. I decided I wanted to combine my love for both art and computers, so I tried to teach myself graphic design. Deciding to make a career out of front end development and graphic design has led me to discover the UI/UX Design track at BLOC. While completing the course, I also obtained my NASM-CPT and became a personal trainer to supplement income.

As a personal trainer, I have built countless workout plans individualized around having to understand what the client needs. Similar to how UX needs to empathize with its users, being a trainer requires an emotional and trusting connection with the client to obtain their end goals.

I've had every type of ailment, problem, or challenge, personal to each prospect, come across my desk. Once that prospect becomes my client, I interview them to gain as much information about who they are and what possible issues they deal with. What energizes me the most is a client with an issue I haven't dealt with before. It's a new challenge that allows me to explore new areas that I haven't before. In order for both the client or myself to be successful, I have to know what obstacles I am dealing with. It's not sarcasm when I say the fun continues every 2 or 3 weeks when I redesign workouts around updated data and other interfering variables. To have continued success, constant iteration of periodized plans is critical, which is exactly like the flow of wireframing and user testing.

Being able to adapt a periodized plan to fit the person's interest and their needs is similar to that of UX design: the user is always in mind but it still needs to be functional.

Being a student at BLOC has only affirmed my decision to make this a career. I've had the opportunity to see my designs come to life on several projects. For example, FitForm is an idea I've had for a fitness app/website. With the skills I've learned, I was able to design and create the website from scratch. Additionally, PBNation is a forum that was in desperate need of a makeover. Not only was it unattractive, it was difficult for users to access the information they needed. With my redesign, users were able to find what they needed in a quarter of the time. Both projects had their unique challenges, but have only increased my excitement for future design opportunities.
